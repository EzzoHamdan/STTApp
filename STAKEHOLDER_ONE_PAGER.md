# STT Notery Public: One-Page Brief for Business Stakeholders

## Executive Summary
STT Notery Public is a practical Arabic speech-to-text platform built for high-stakes conversations where every word matters. It captures live speech, turns it into structured text in real time, and produces a clear, unified transcript that can be reviewed, searched, and shared.

What makes this project special is not only transcription. It gives decision-makers confidence. The platform combines two business-ready capabilities: a Court Session mode for multi-speaker proceedings and an Arena mode that compares multiple speech engines side by side. This means your team can move from guesswork to evidence when choosing quality, speed, and cost.

## The Business Problem It Solves
Many organizations still depend on manual note-taking during meetings, hearings, and formal sessions. That creates real risk:
- Critical details can be missed.
- Human notes vary in quality.
- Follow-up takes too long.
- Disputes become harder to resolve without a strong record.

For Arabic-speaking environments, this challenge is even bigger because model quality differs widely by provider, dialect, and speaking style. Choosing the wrong provider can waste budget and reduce trust in the output.

## What This Platform Delivers (In Plain Language)
### 1) Court Session Mode
- Supports multiple named speakers in one live session (for example: Judge, Lawyer 1, Lawyer 2).
- Builds a single timeline of who said what and when.
- Flags moments where people talk over each other.
- Produces clean export files for reporting, archiving, or review.

Business outcome: better transparency, stronger documentation, and faster post-session turnaround.

### 2) STT Arena Mode
- Streams the same audio to several Arabic STT providers at the same time.
- Shows outputs side by side so your team can compare results live.
- Helps you evaluate quality, latency, and reliability before committing spend.

Business outcome: smarter vendor decisions, lower lock-in risk, and better return on technology investment.

## Why It Is Worth a Serious Shot
### Faster operations with less manual overhead
Teams can spend less time writing notes and more time making decisions. The system captures and organizes speech continuously, reducing rework after sessions.

### Better accountability and audit readiness
Every session is stored with timestamps and structured entries. This strengthens internal governance and supports review when questions appear later.

### Vendor flexibility is built in
The platform is designed to work across multiple speech providers, not just one. If pricing, quality, or service reliability changes, your organization can adapt quickly.

### Practical security controls for controlled access
An access code gate can protect usage, and provider keys are managed in a controlled way. This supports safer pilots and phased rollout.

### Low-friction deployment
The project can run in modern web environments and is already structured for deployment workflows. This lowers time-to-value and helps teams start small without heavy infrastructure programs.

## Why Now
Waiting has a hidden cost. Every month of manual transcription means slower operations, inconsistent records, and avoidable administrative burden. This project is already beyond idea stage: it is a functioning system with live capture, multi-provider integration, and exportable outputs.

A pilot now gives leadership real data on quality, timing, and cost with limited risk. Instead of committing to a large program up front, you can validate value quickly and scale only when the evidence is strong.

## Recommended Pilot Approach (30 Days)
- Week 1: Configure keys and run internal dry sessions.
- Week 2: Run live sessions in one controlled team.
- Week 3: Compare providers in Arena mode and identify best-fit options.
- Week 4: Report business impact and define scale-up plan.

## Success Metrics to Track
- Time saved per session versus manual process.
- Transcript usability score from end users.
- Turnaround time from session end to final transcript.
- Error and overlap review rate.
- Cost per recorded hour by provider.
